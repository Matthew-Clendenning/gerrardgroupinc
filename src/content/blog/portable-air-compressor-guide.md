Portable diesel air compressors are essential for pipeline work, mining, drilling, sandblasting, and large-scale construction. Atlas Copco and Sullair are two of the most trusted names in the industry. If you're shopping for a used unit, here's what you need to know.

## High Pressure vs High Volume: Know What You Need

Before comparing brands, understand the two main categories:

**High-pressure compressors** (300+ PSI) are designed for:
- Pipeline testing and purging
- Well drilling and well service
- High-pressure sandblasting
- Mining operations

**High-volume, medium-pressure compressors** (100–150 PSI) are designed for:
- General sandblasting
- Pneumatic tool operation (jackhammers, impact wrenches)
- Pipeline blowing and drying
- Construction site air supply

Choosing the wrong type means either insufficient pressure for the job or paying for capacity you don't need.

## What to Check When Buying Used

Regardless of brand, inspect these items on any used portable compressor:

1. **Hours** — under 2,000 hours is considered low usage; over 5,000 warrants closer inspection of wear items
2. **Engine condition** — check for smoke, unusual noise, oil leaks, and coolant condition
3. **Air end** — the compressor element itself; listen for bearing noise and check discharge temperatures
4. **Oil separator** — a clogged or failed separator causes oil carryover into the air stream
5. **Coolers** — blocked or damaged after coolers reduce efficiency and can cause overheating
6. **Frame and tires** — check for structural damage, tire condition, brake function, and axle wear
7. **Control panel** — all gauges, shutdowns, and safety systems should be functional
